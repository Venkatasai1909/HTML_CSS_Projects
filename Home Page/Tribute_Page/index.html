Dr.A.P.J.Abdul Kalam

Missile Man of India

Events in Abdul Kalam's life

  • 1931 - Born as Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India.

  • 1950 - Enrolled at St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, to pursue a degree in physics.

  • 1954 - Graduated with a Bachelor of Science degree in Physics from the University of Madras.

  • 1955 - Joined the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT) to study aerospace engineering.

  • 1960 - Completed his Master of Science degree in Aerospace Engineering from MIT.

  • 1962 - Joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) as a scientist.

  • 1969 - Transferred to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and played a significant role in developing India's space program.

  • 1980 - Served as the Chief Executive of the Integrated Guided Missile Development Program (IGMDP) and led the development of India's indigenous ballistic missiles, Agni and Prithvi.

  • 1992 - Appointed as the Scientific Advisor to the Defence Minister of India.

  • 1997 - Awarded the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, for his contributions to science and space research.

  • 1997-2001- Served as the 11th President of India, becoming the first scientist and bachelor to hold the position.

  • 2002-2007- Presidential term as the President of India.

  • 2009 - Inducted into the Indian Space Research Organisation's Satellite Centre, Bengaluru, named as the "ISRO Propulsion Complex" in his honor.
